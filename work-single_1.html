Cathartic Virtualization


The series of works "Cathartic Virtualization," created by visual artist Amanda Teixeira de Oliveira, expresses the moment from trauma to healing. When a traumatic event occurs, we lack the full vision to process it, just as with the square units, pixelated abstractions that comprise the works.

Observing the works closely, we are unable to grasp the image that can be formed. This is also the case with traumas. When we distance ourselves from the events to analyze them from the outside, as an object of analysis, distancing ourselves from the victim's position, or when we distance ourselves in time, we can process them with a view of the whole, and thus, healing can occur.

Starting from the premise that thoughts are virtual, the ability to process traumatic events culminates in healing through cathartic virtualization. From a distance, the works utilize the Gestalt law—proximity—to perceive the figuration of the image.

Memes were used as the image formed by pixels because they possess the symbolic expressiveness of contemporary culture. Just as Don Juan is the ephemeral lover, in contemporary times we have Giga Chad as the symbol of the "Alpha" man. Just as in biology, culture has its gene, the meme, which is the particle that expresses culture as its multiplying capacity, meaning it can be applied in different contexts.

The completion of a healing process from emotional trauma, in popular belief, previously expressed with the phrase "In the future we will laugh about this," is a fundamental characteristic of memes: humor.
